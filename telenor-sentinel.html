Vulnerability Management & Least Privilege Auditing

Enterprise security governance mandates that unverified accounts and excessive user permissions remain the primary attack vectors in telecommunications IT infrastructure. By executing systemic **Access Reviews** and actively enforcing the **Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)**, Telenor prevents lateral movement in the event of an account compromise.

Key Compliance Standard (ISO/IEC 27001:2022 A.8.2):
Privileged access rights must be restricted and controlled. Regular user permission reviews prevent administrative creep—where developers and third-party vendors retain high-privilege credentials long after active tasks close.

PTA Cybersecurity framework Compliance and Fines

Under the Pakistan Telecommunication Authority (PTA) cybersecurity guidelines, telecommunication operators are liable to severe operational audits and financial penalties for neglecting critical vulnerabilities or failing to secure signaling gateways (SS7, Diameter protocols).

By coupling direct **Vulnerability Mitigation** (patching outdated OpenSSL/TLS switches) with automated controls like **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)**, security teams reduce the risk of signaling compromises. Proving security posture audit readiness visually through interactive SOC simulation HUDs demonstrates advanced technical understanding and operational agility.
