📚 Telenor northern sector energy strategy briefing

DC-Powered Solar & Hybrid HVAC Architecture

Traditional telecom sites suffer high conversion losses converting DC battery power to AC for air conditioning, then rectifying AC grid power back to DC for transceivers. By deploying native **DC-powered variable-capacity air conditioners**, Telenor avoids up to 18% inverter conversion losses. Combining this with high-efficiency solar PV arrays allows Margalla and urban towers to run completely off-grid during daylight hours.

Key Planning Strategy (DC Direct Coupling):
Solar panels feed energy directly into the DC common bus bar, charging Lithium-Ion battery banks and running transceivers directly. The smart generator only fires as a final safety trigger when battery SoC drops below critical thresholds.

OPEX Sensitivities & World Bank SDG Alignment

Telenor's network cost model is highly sensitive to remote site diesel delivery tariffs. Incorporating solar sizing and battery storage calculations into a unified data optimization model allows planning teams to design custom regional power designs. Right-sizing battery storage from 40kWh to 80kWh in cloudy high-altitude sectors avoids thermal runaway risks and significantly drops diesel generator maintenance costs.

This energy planning methodology supports **SDG 7 (Affordable & Clean Energy)** and **SDG 13 (Climate Action)**. Proving these sensitivities through interactive data-driven models demonstrates advanced engineering operations research and strategic cost management.
