Mudarabah (Profit-Sharing)

One partner (Rab-ul-Maal) provides full capital, and the other (Mudarib) provides expertise and labor. Profits are shared by pre-agreed ratios, while losses are strictly borne by the capital provider.

Musharakah (Joint Venture)

Joint venture partnership where both bank and customer contribute capital. Profits are shared as agreed, while losses are shared strictly in proportion to capital contributions.

Murabaha (Cost-Plus Sale)

The bank buys an asset and sells it to the customer at a cost plus profit markup. Payment is made in customized deferred installments.
